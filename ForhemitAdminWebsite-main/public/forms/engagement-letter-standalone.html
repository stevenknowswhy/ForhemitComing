§ 01Parties and Nature of Engagement

This Engagement Letter ("Agreement") is entered into between Forhemit Stewardship Management Co., a California Public Benefit Corporation, doing business as Forhemit Transition Stewardship ("Forhemit"), and [Company] ("Company"), acting through [Authorized Officer].

Forhemit is a transaction stewardship firm that facilitates employee ownership transitions — coordinating the transaction team, managing the process timeline, preparing the lender and trustee package, and providing post-close operational stewardship for a period of 12 to 24 months following closing.

IMPORTANT: Forhemit is not a buyer, investor, lender, broker, attorney, or financial advisor. The ESOP trust is the legal acquirer. All Forhemit fees are paid by the Company as ordinary operating business expenses.

ERISA DISCLAIMER: Forhemit is NOT an ERISA fiduciary. All ERISA matters must be reviewed by independent ERISA counsel engaged and directed solely by the ESOP trustee.

TAX DISCLAIMER: Forhemit does NOT provide tax advice. All tax matters, including §1042 election analysis, must be reviewed by independent tax professionals.
§ 02Scope of Services

Forhemit's engagement encompasses the following services across a structured 120-day transaction process, organized into five phases with four Hard Stop gates:

01Transaction Coordination — Assembly and management of the full professional team including trustee, ERISA counsel, appraiser, QofE provider, and SBA lender.
02Lender Package Preparation — Compilation of financial, operational, and legal documentation required for SBA 7(a) underwriting and trustee review.
03COOP Development — Continuity of Operations Plan across four progressive versions (v1–v4) from Day 11 through Day 90, culminating in trustee sign-off at Gate 4.
04Process Management — Timeline governance including four Hard Stop gates at Days 11, 35, 63, and 90.
05Post-Close Stewardship — Operational support for 12–24 months beginning Day 121, at the Annual Stewardship Fee set forth in §04.

Services do not include legal representation, tax advice, ERISA fiduciary services, brokerage, investment advice, or capital provision of any kind.

The Retainer is earned in full upon execution of this Agreement. It does not apply toward the Transition Success Fee or any other fee obligation. The Retainer compensates Forhemit for pre-assessment work, professional team coordination, deal structuring time, and advisor engagement that commences immediately upon execution.

Non-Refundable: The Retainer is non-refundable in all circumstances except (a) Forhemit's material breach, or (b) Forhemit's failure to substantially commence material services within 10 business days of the payment due date. In no event shall the Retainer be refunded due to deal failure, lender decline, trustee non-approval, or Company's decision not to proceed.

The Retainer is fully refundable for a period of 30 calendar days following execution ("Refund Period"), provided Company delivers written notice to deals@forhemit.com prior to expiration. Following expiration, the Retainer is earned and non-refundable.

Refundable Period Active: Company may terminate without cause and receive a full refund if written notice is delivered within the Refund Period.

§ 06Confidentiality

Each Party agrees to hold in strict confidence all non-public information disclosed in connection with this engagement. This obligation survives termination for three (3) years.

§ 07Term and Termination

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues through closing and Post-Close Stewardship. Either Party may terminate upon 15 days' written notice. Forhemit may terminate immediately upon material breach, fraud, or non-payment. Upon termination: (a) earned fees remain due; (b) Retainer governed by §03; (c) tail provision in §08 applies.
