Instructions: Work through each phase in order. Check off items as completed. All employee-facing materials must be reviewed by ERISA counsel before distribution. Coordinate with the trustee on the welcome letter. Log all feedback in the Employee Feedback Tracker post-announcement.
§ 01Pre-Announcement Planning
T-30 to T-7 days before announcement
Identify key employee communicators. Select 2–3 senior leaders who will serve as visible points of contact during and after the announcement.
Draft announcement timeline. Set the date, time, and sequence: town hall first, then written announcement, then ESOP booklet distribution.
Draft employee announcement letter. Use the Employee Announcement template (#62). Customize for company voice.
Draft manager briefing talking points. Use the Manager Briefing Talking Points template (#137). Include Q&A for common employee concerns.
Prepare employee FAQ document. Cover: “What is an ESOP?”, “Do I have to buy shares?”, “When do I get money?”, “What if I leave?”, “Is my 401(k) affected?”
Prepare “Welcome to Your ESOP” booklet. Use the Welcome to Your ESOP Booklet template (#138). Customize with company-specific data.
Request trustee welcome letter. Coordinate with the independent trustee to prepare a letter to all employees explaining their rights and protections.
Schedule town hall meeting. Book the room or video link. Set the agenda using the ESOP Town Hall Agenda template (#123).
Coordinate with seller/owner. Confirm the owner is prepared to speak at the town hall. Review their talking points.
§ 02Legal & Trustee Review
T-14 to T-3 days before announcement
All employee materials reviewed by ERISA counsel. Employee announcement, FAQ, ESOP booklet, and talking points must be approved for legal accuracy.
Trustee welcome letter approved. Trustee reviews and approves their own letter. Confirm delivery method and timing.
Management talking points reviewed. ERISA counsel confirms talking points do not make prohibited promises (specific stock values, guaranteed returns, timelines).
Q&A responses vetted. All pre-drafted answers to anticipated employee questions reviewed for accuracy and legal compliance.
Confidentiality compliance confirmed. Verify that pre-announcement information has been properly restricted. No employee should learn about the ESOP before the official announcement.
Tax disclaimers included. All materials include appropriate language: “Consult your tax advisor for personal implications.”
§ 03Logistics
T-7 to T-1 days before announcement
Town hall room or video link confirmed. Test AV equipment. Ensure capacity for all employees or plan multiple sessions.
ESOP booklet printed or loaded. Have physical copies available at the town hall and a digital version ready for email distribution.
Benefit statement samples ready. Prepare mock annual benefit statements so employees understand what they’ll receive each year.
HR briefed on individual questions. HR should be prepared to handle one-on-one questions after the announcement. Provide HR with a cheat sheet of approved answers.
IT prepared for intranet posting. If the company has an intranet or internal communications channel, prepare the announcement for posting immediately after the town hall.
Catering ordered (if applicable). A light reception after the town hall reinforces the celebratory nature of the announcement.
Materials staged in the meeting room. Announcements, booklets, and any handouts placed at each seat or at a distribution table.
§ 04Announcement Day
T-0 — Day of announcement
Town hall executed. Follow the ESOP Town Hall Agenda. Owner speaks first, then facilitator leads Q&A. Distribute materials at the meeting.
Employee announcement distributed. Written announcement sent to all employees (email and/or printed) immediately after the town hall.
Trustee welcome letter distributed. Trustee’s letter included with or immediately following the employee announcement.
ESOP booklet distributed. “Welcome to Your ESOP” booklet provided to every employee. Digital version posted to intranet.
Management available for questions. Key communicators have cleared their schedules for the remainder of the day to handle individual questions.
Feedback collection started. Place a feedback form (physical or digital) for employees who prefer to ask questions anonymously.
§ 05Post-Announcement Follow-Up
T+1 to T+30 days after announcement
Collect initial employee questions. Aggregate questions from the town hall, HR, anonymous feedback, and direct conversations.
Schedule follow-up Q&A sessions. Offer department-level or small group sessions for employees who have more questions after the initial announcement.
Distribute first annual benefit statement (when available). Use the Annual Benefit Statement Cover Letter template (#139) to accompany the first statement.
Begin ownership culture workshop series. Schedule the first quarterly workshop using the Ownership Culture Workshop Guide template (#140).
Log all feedback in Employee Feedback Tracker. Record questions, concerns, positive comments, and sentiment indicators for the next Forhemit stewardship review.
Report to Forhemit stewardship lead. Share a summary of the announcement execution, employee sentiment, and any issues that arose.
§ 06Sign-Off

This checklist is complete. All employee communication materials have been prepared, reviewed, and distributed according to the strategy.
