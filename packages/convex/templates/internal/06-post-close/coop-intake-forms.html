§ 01Track 1 — People

This track captures the organizational and human capital information needed to begin stewardship. Complete all items within the first 30 days of the stewardship engagement.

Employee Census — Full roster of all employees as of the stewardship start date.
☐Employee roster — Full name, title, department, hire date, annual compensation, and ESOP eligibility status for all employees. {{employeeCount}} total employees.
☐Org chart — Current organizational chart showing reporting structure, departments, and headcount by function.
☐Key personnel profiles — Detailed profiles for the top 5-7 leaders: tenure, role, responsibilities, compensation, retention risk assessment.
Training Needs Assessment — Evaluate current training infrastructure and ESOP education gaps.
☐Current training programs — Inventory of existing training, onboarding, and professional development programs. Identify gaps relative to ESOP stewardship requirements.
☐ESOP education plan — Develop employee education plan covering: what an ESOP is, how accounts grow, vesting schedules, distribution rules, and participant rights under ERISA.
☐Management development — Identify management training needs for the post-owner leadership team. Focus areas: financial literacy, decision-making frameworks, open-book management.
ESOP Committee Formation — Establish the internal governance structure for ESOP administration.
☐ESOP committee charter — Draft charter defining: committee purpose, membership (3-5 members), meeting frequency, reporting structure, and authority.
☐Committee member selection — Identify and appoint initial committee members. Must include representation from management and non-management employees.
☐First committee meeting — Schedule and conduct the inaugural ESOP committee meeting. Agenda: review charter, set calendar, discuss communication plan.
Communication Plan — Establish ongoing employee communication about the ESOP and company performance.
☐ESOP announcement communication — Prepare and distribute the initial ESOP announcement to all employees. Include: what changed, what it means, next steps, FAQ.
☐Annual meeting plan — Establish the annual ESOP meeting format, timing, and content requirements (account statements, company performance review, Q&A).
☐Ongoing communication cadence — Define quarterly or monthly communication touchpoints: newsletters, town halls, performance dashboards, open-book metrics.

§ 02Track 2 — Systems

This track captures the technology and operational systems information needed for stewardship. Complete all items within the first 60 days of the stewardship engagement.

Current Systems Assessment — Inventory of all business-critical technology systems.
☐Technology stack inventory — Complete list of all software, SaaS platforms, and on-premise systems. Include: vendor, license count, annual cost, contract renewal date, and criticality rating.
☐IT infrastructure review — Document servers, networking, cloud hosting, backup systems, and disaster recovery capabilities.
☐Cybersecurity posture — Assess current security controls: MFA, endpoint protection, training, incident response plan, insurance coverage.
ERP / Accounting Review — Evaluate financial systems readiness for post-close reporting.
☐Accounting system — Identify current accounting platform (QuickBooks, Sage, NetSuite, other). Assess capabilities for: multi-entity reporting, ESOP accounting, ASC 715 pension accounting.
☐Chart of accounts review — Evaluate chart of accounts structure. Determine if modifications are needed for ESOP-related accounts (ESOP liability, repurchase obligation, contributed surplus).
☐Financial reporting cadence — Define monthly close process, management reporting package, and board reporting requirements.
HR / Benefits Review — Assess human resources infrastructure and benefits administration.
☐HR platform — Identify current HRIS, payroll provider, and benefits administration platform. Assess integration capabilities with ESOP recordkeeper.
☐Current benefit plans — Inventory all employee benefit plans: health, dental, vision, 401(k)/profit-sharing, life, disability, HSA/FSA. Include carrier, renewal date, and employee contribution rates.
☐ESOP recordkeeper coordination — Confirm ESOP recordkeeper selection. Establish data feed requirements between payroll, HRIS, and the recordkeeper.
Governance Structure — Establish the formal governance framework for the ESOP-owned company.
☐Board of directors — Confirm board composition. Document board meeting cadence, quorum requirements, and fiduciary duties under ERISA and state law.
☐Officer roles and responsibilities — Document officer positions (CEO, CFO, COO), reporting lines, and decision-making authority. Clarify any seller transition roles.
☐Policy manual — Review and update corporate policies: code of ethics, conflict of interest, related-party transactions, compensation philosophy, distribution policy.
