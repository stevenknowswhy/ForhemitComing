§ 01Confidentiality Obligations

This is your quarterly reminder on confidentiality. Every Forhemit team member handles confidential deal information. Here are the rules — they apply at all times, not just during this quarter.

The core rule: If you haven't been explicitly told you can share it, don't. Deal information stays within the deal team and authorized parties only.
§ 02What Is Confidential
A.Company identity. The name of any company in the pipeline, in transaction, or in stewardship. Never mention a company name to anyone outside the deal team without written authorization.
B.Financial information. Revenue, EBITDA, valuation, purchase price, capital stack, fee structure — all confidential. Never share financials outside the deal team.
C.Deal status. Whether a deal is in pre-flight, in transaction, or closed. Don't confirm or deny deal status to anyone outside the deal team.
D.Personal information. Seller's personal financial situation, tax details, estate planning — all confidential and privileged.
E.Team information. Who the trustee, counsel, appraiser, or lender is — not public information. Don't share the deal team roster.
§ 03Common Mistakes
1.Referring by name. "We're working with [company name]" — even to other brokers, CPAs, or attorneys not on the deal team. Use the reference number only: "We have an active deal, ref FH-2026-XXXX."
2.Discussing in public. Deals discussed in coffee shops, airports, or restaurants can be overheard. Use neutral language or wait until you're in a private setting.
3.Forwarding emails. Never forward a deal email to anyone not on the deal team. If someone needs information, send a summary or ask them to contact Forhemit directly.
4.Social media. Never post about deals, deal activity, or client relationships on LinkedIn, Twitter, or any public platform — even without naming the company.
5.Family and friends. "I'm working on an ESOP deal for a manufacturing company" — even vague details can identify the company to someone in the industry. Don't discuss deals outside of work.
§ 04Exceptions

You may share deal information only in these situations:

A.Deal team members. Anyone listed on the deal team roster for that specific deal.
B.Authorized third parties. Brokers, CPAs, or attorneys who have been explicitly authorized by the seller or Forhemit management.
C.Forhemit management. Managing partners and designated oversight personnel.
D.Legal requirement. If compelled by law, court order, or regulatory authority — notify Forhemit management immediately before disclosing.
When in doubt, ask. If you're not sure whether you can share something, don't share it. Ask your manager or Forhemit management. The cost of asking is zero. The cost of a confidentiality breach is the deal — and potentially the firm.
§ 05Acknowledgment

By continuing to work on Forhemit deals, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and will comply with these confidentiality obligations. If you have questions about what is or isn't confidential, ask before you share.
