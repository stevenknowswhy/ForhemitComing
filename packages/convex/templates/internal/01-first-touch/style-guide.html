§ 01Voice & Tone

All Forhemit communications aim for a consistent voice: professional but warm, knowledgeable but accessible. We are stewards guiding business owners through the most important financial decision of their lives. Every sentence should reflect that responsibility.

A.Plain language first. Write at an 8th-grade reading level. If a simpler word exists, use it. Replace “utilize” with “use,” “facilitate” with “help,” “subsequently” with “then.”
B.Explain jargon on first use. ESOP, FMV, QofE, DSCR — spell out the term, give a one-sentence explanation, then use the abbreviation. Never assume the reader knows the acronym.
C.Empathetic but direct. Acknowledge the seller’s emotional investment without being patronizing. Avoid “I understand this is hard” — instead, make the hard thing clear and actionable.
D.Active voice. “We will review the documents” not “The documents will be reviewed.” Active voice is shorter, clearer, and feels more accountable.
E.Positive framing. Lead with what is possible, not what is prohibited. “The SBA requires a minimum DSCR of 1.25x” rather than “You cannot proceed without meeting the DSCR threshold.”
§ 02Formatting Rules

Consistency across documents builds trust. Every Forhemit document follows these structural conventions.

A.Document header. Every document opens with the Forhemit brand (left) and contact info (right), separated by a brass line. Reference numbers appear in the header.
B.Cover grid. A 2×2 grid below the title contains: Company/Seller, Date, Reference, and Status. This anchors every document with the same metadata.
C.Numbered sections. Use section headers with monospace section numbers (§ 01, § 02, etc.) and display-font titles. Each section is self-contained and can be read independently.
D.Callout boxes. Use brass left-border callouts for important notes, warnings, or context that deserves visual emphasis. Keep callouts to 1–2 sentences.
E.Numbered lists. Use lettered sub-items (A, B, C) within sections and numbered items (1, 2, 3) for sequential steps or ordered priorities.
F.Signature blocks. Agreements end with a dual-column signature block: Forhemit (left) and Date (right). Transmittals end with a standard footer only.

§ 04Email Conventions

Emails are the primary communication channel during the transaction. Consistency builds trust and makes searching easy.

A.Subject line format. [Forhemit] — Document Name — Company Name. Example: [Forhemit] — Pre-flight Checklist — Acme Manufacturing.
B.Greeting. Use the recipient’s first name. “Hi Sarah,” not “Dear Ms. Johnson” and not “Hey.” For groups, use “Team,” or list names.
C.Body. Lead with the purpose in the first sentence. Keep paragraphs to 2–3 sentences maximum. Use bullet points for action items.
D.Sign-off. Use “Best,” or “Thanks,” followed by first name. For formal documents, use “Sincerely,” with full name and title.
E.Reference number. Always include the Forhemit reference (FHM-YYYY-###) in the body, typically as “Ref: FHM-2026-042” near the top of the email.
F.Confidentiality footer. All emails to external parties include the standard Forhemit confidentiality notice at the bottom.
§ 05Document Types

Forhemit documents fall into five categories. Each type has a distinct purpose and formatting expectation.

A.Transmittals. Cover emails or memos that accompany a primary document. They explain what is enclosed, why it matters, and what the recipient should do next. Short — typically under 200 words.
B.Agreements. Binding or non-binding documents that establish terms: engagement letters, LOIs, NDAs, retainer agreements, intercreditor agreements. Include signature blocks and governing law.
C.Calculators. Interactive financial models with input fields, scenario tables, and summary outputs. Examples: net proceeds calculator, turnover cost calculator, deal economics model.
D.Status updates. Recurring progress reports sent to sellers, brokers, lenders, and trustees during diligence. Include gate milestones, completed items, and next actions.
E.Confirmations. One-time notifications of a completed event: gate passage, closing date, commission payment, wire instructions. Short and factual.
§ 06Numerical Formatting

Numbers carry weight in financial communications. Format them consistently to avoid ambiguity.

A.Currency. Use the dollar sign and comma separators: $1,250,000. For millions, use $1.25M. Never use $1.25mm or $1,250K.
B.Percentages. Use one decimal place for rates: 6.5%. Use two decimals for tax rates: 23.8%. Always include the % symbol.
C.Dates. Use Month D, YYYY format: May 24, 2026. Never use 05/24/2026 or 24-May-26 in documents. In emails, the same format applies.
D.Reference numbers. FHM-YYYY-### format: FHM-2026-042. Always include the prefix. Zero-pad to three digits.
E.Ratios. Use the “x” suffix with one decimal: 4.5x multiple, 1.25x DSCR. Do not write “4.5 times” in formal documents.
F.Time periods. Spell out: “12 months,” “120 days,” “24 months.” Do not abbreviate to “12 mo.” or “120d.”
§ 07Prohibited Language

These words and phrases undermine credibility or create legal risk. Eliminate them from all Forhemit communications.

“Obviously”Condescending. If it were obvious, you wouldn’t need to say it.
“Just”Minimizes complexity. “Just sign here” ignores the gravity of the decision.
“Simply”Same as “just” — minimizes what is often a complex step.
“Trust me”Trust is earned through transparency, not asserted. Show the numbers instead.
“Guarantee”Creates legal liability. We cannot guarantee outcomes — we can only present qualified analysis.
“Best deal”Subjective and unverifiable. Use “favorable structure” or “competitive terms” instead.
“You should”Prescriptive. Use “We recommend” or “The typical approach is” to inform without directing.
Test: Read the sentence aloud. If it sounds like something a car salesperson would say, rewrite it. We are stewards, not closers.
§ 08Legal Considerations

Every Forhemit document is a reflection of our fiduciary responsibility. These rules protect the seller, the company, and Forhemit.

A.No promises of outcomes. We present scenarios, models, and qualified opinions — never guarantees of specific results, tax savings, or valuations.
B.Distinguish advice from information. Provide information (“The §1042 election allows deferral of capital gains”). Do not provide tax or legal advice (“You should elect §1042”). Always direct sellers to their own CPA and counsel.
C.Include appropriate disclaimers. Financial calculators note they are indicative only. Documents involving legal terms note they are subject to counsel review. Templates note they are frameworks, not final documents.
D.Confidentiality. Every document includes a confidentiality notice. Documents shared in the deal room stay in the deal room. Never reference one seller’s deal terms with another seller.
E.ERISA boundaries. Forhemit is not the trustee, not ERISA counsel, and not the appraiser. We coordinate but never direct the independent fiduciaries. Language must reflect this separation.
