## 1. CRITICAL RISKS (🔴 Immediate Attention Required)

### 1.1 Undefined "Substantially Assembled" in Tail Provision
**Severity**: 🔴 CRITICAL | **Affects**: Client Company

The 180-day tail provision triggers fees if a transaction closes "with a team substantially assembled by Forhemit." This phrase is **undefined and highly subjective** — a litigation trap waiting to happen.

**Current Language**:
> "If the company closes a transaction within 180 days of termination with a team substantially assembled by Forhemit, the success fee is owed."

**Issues**:
- No objective standard for "substantially assembled"
- No causation requirement — fee owed even if Forhemit had no involvement in eventual transaction
- No notice mechanism for potential tail transactions
- Fee not reduced if Forhemit performs no work post-termination

**Recommended Fix**:
```
"For purposes of this provision, a team is 'substantially assembled by Forhemit' only if
(a) Forhemit introduced at least fifty percent (50%) of the ultimately-selected transaction
professionals (ESOP counsel, trustee, valuation firm, and lender), and (b) such introductions
are documented in writing by Forhemit within ten (10) business days of such introduction, and
(c) no successor advisor is engaged for any position where Forhemit's introduction was rejected
by Company."

Additionally: "The tail provision shall not apply if the engagement is terminated by Company
for Forhemit's material breach or willful misconduct."
```

### 1.2 No Material Breach Exception to Non-Refundable Retainer
**Severity**: 🔴 CRITICAL | **Affects**: Client Company

The $25,000 retainer is "non-refundable under all circumstances" — even if Forhemit materially breaches or fails to perform. This may be **unenforceable as an unlawful forfeiture** in many jurisdictions.

**Current Language**:
> "The Retainer is earned in full at execution. It does not apply toward the Transition Success Fee or any other fee. The Retainer is non-refundable under all circumstances, including if the transaction does not close for any reason."

**Issues**:
- "Execution" ambiguous — agreement execution vs. closing execution
- "Under all circumstances" may be unenforceable as unconscionable
- No carve-out for Forhemit material breach
- No pro-rating if Forhemit fails to commence services

**Legal Principles**:
- Forfeiture doctrine: Some courts refuse to enforce "no refund" provisions where they constitute unlawful forfeiture
- Unconscionability: "Under all circumstances" may be vulnerable to challenge

**Recommended Fix**:
```
"The Retainer in the amount of $25,000 shall be deemed earned by Forhemit upon execution of this
Agreement by both parties and shall be non-refundable, except in the event that Forhemit
materially breaches this Agreement or fails to substantially perform the services contemplated
herein. Notwithstanding the foregoing, if this Agreement is terminated by the Company prior to
Forhemit's commencement of material services, the Retainer shall be pro-rated and the unearned
portion refunded to the Company."
```

### 1.4 Inadequate §1042 Tax Election Warning
**Severity**: 🔴 CRITICAL | **Affects**: Both Parties

The S-Corp conversion warning fails to explain the permanent tax consequences of converting to C-Corp status for §1042 eligibility.

**Current Warning**:
> "S-Corporation structures require conversion to C-Corporation status prior to closing to
qualify for the §1042 election. This conversion has legal and tax consequences that must be
reviewed by the Company's CPA and ERISA counsel before LOI."

**Deficiencies**:
1. Does not explain what §1042 is or why it matters
2. Does not warn about timing constraints
3. Does not address permanent loss of S-Corp status
4. References "ERISA counsel" for what is primarily a tax matter (§1042 is IRC, not ERISA)

**Recommended Replacement**:
```
"WARNING: Section 1042 of the Internal Revenue Code allows qualifying sellers to defer capital
gains tax by reinvesting proceeds in Qualified Replacement Property (QRP), BUT ONLY if the
selling entity is a C-Corporation at the time of sale. S-Corporations do NOT qualify.

Converting from S-Corp to C-Corp to access §1042 has PERMANENT tax consequences:
(1) the company becomes subject to double taxation (corporate + shareholder) on all future
    earnings;
(2) built-in gains tax may apply;
(3) pass-through losses are eliminated;
(4) state-level tax treatment changes.

This conversion is IRREVERSIBLE and should ONLY be undertaken after consultation with QUALIFIED
TAX COUNSEL and a THOROUGH ANALYSIS of whether §1042 benefits outweigh the permanent C-Corp
tax burden. Forhemit does NOT provide tax advice and makes NO recommendation regarding §1042
election or S-Corp conversion."
```
