### [ ] 1.1 Tail Provision — Define "Substantially Assembled"

**Current Location**: Section 7 hint text (line 563)
**Current Language**: "If the company closes a transaction within 180 days of termination with a team substantially assembled by Forhemit, the success fee is owed."

**Issues**:
- "Substantially assembled" is undefined and subjective
- No causation requirement
- No notice mechanism
- Creates significant litigation risk

**Recommended Revision** (counsel to adapt):
```
"Tail Provision. If the Company closes a transaction with any person or entity that (i) was
introduced to the Company by Forhemit during the term of this Agreement, or (ii) was identified
as a prospective buyer or investor by Forhemit during the term, within 180 days following the
termination or expiration of this Agreement, then the Transition Success Fee shall be payable
as if this Agreement had remained in effect through the closing date, provided that Forhemit
shall have provided the Company with written notice of such introduction or identification
prior to termination. For the avoidance of doubt, a transaction with a party not introduced or
identified by Forhemit shall not trigger this Tail Provision, regardless of whether Forhemit
assembled a team or performed services for the Company."
```

**Attorney Notes**:
- [ ] Confirm 180-day period is appropriate for ESOP transactions
- [ ] Consider whether to carve out termination for cause
- [ ] Verify notice mechanism is workable

### [ ] 1.4 Expand §1042 Tax Election Warning

**Current Location**: Section 5 scorp-warn div (line 448-450)
**Current Language**: Basic warning only

**Issues**:
- Does not explain permanent consequences of S-Corp to C-Corp conversion
- Does not adequately warn about tax implications
- References "ERISA counsel" for tax matter (incorrect)

**Recommended Revision** (counsel to adapt):
```
"WARNING: Section 1042 of the Internal Revenue Code allows qualifying sellers to defer
capital gains tax by reinvesting proceeds in Qualified Replacement Property (QRP), BUT
ONLY if the selling entity is a C-Corporation at the time of sale. S-Corporations do NOT
qualify.

Converting from S-Corp to C-Corp to access §1042 has PERMANENT tax consequences:
(1) the company becomes subject to double taxation (corporate + shareholder) on all future
    earnings;
(2) built-in gains tax may apply;
(3) pass-through losses are eliminated;
(4) state-level tax treatment changes.

This conversion is IRREVERSIBLE and should ONLY be undertaken after consultation with
QUALIFIED TAX COUNSEL and a THOROUGH ANALYSIS of whether §1042 benefits outweigh the
permanent C-Corp tax burden. Forhemit does NOT provide tax advice and makes NO
recommendation regarding §1042 election or S-Corp conversion."
```

**Attorney Notes**:
- [ ] Verify tax accuracy of this warning
- [ ] Advise on any additional tax warnings needed
- [ ] Consider whether this should be a separate document signed by client
