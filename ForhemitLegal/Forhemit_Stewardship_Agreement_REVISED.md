## ARTICLE 3 — STEWARDSHIP FEE

**3.1 Fee Calculation.** The Company shall pay Forhemit an annual stewardship fee equal to **2.5%** of the Quality of Earnings ("QofE") Adjusted EBITDA as of the Closing Date of $___________, which equals $___________ per year (the "Annual Fee"). The Quarterly Fee is $___________.

**3.2 Fee Schedule — Tranche Structure.** The Fee shall be payable in the following tranches:

| Tranche | Period | % of Annual Fee | Amount | Due Upon |
|---------|--------|-----------------|--------|----------|
| Tranche 1 | Months 1–6 | 60% | $___________ | Execution |
| Tranche 2 | Months 7–12 | 20% | $___________ | Month 6 |
| Tranche 3 | Months 13–18 | 10% | $___________ | Written election |
| Tranche 4 | Months 19–24 | 10% | $___________ | Written election |

**3.3 Payment Terms.** All invoices are due and payable within **thirty (30) days** of receipt.

**3.4 Late Payment Interest.** Fees not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of **1.5% per month** (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less) from the due date until paid in full.

**3.5 Suspension of Services.** If any invoice remains unpaid thirty (30) days after receipt, Forhemit may suspend all services until payment is received in full, without liability to the Company. Forhemit may also withhold delivery of the Final Transition Report pending payment in full of all outstanding fees.

**3.6 Expenses.** The Fee does not include out-of-pocket expenses. Reasonable out-of-pocket expenses (including travel and third-party costs) shall be reimbursed by the Company upon presentation of receipts. Expenses exceeding $___________ require prior written approval from the Company.

**3.7 Fee Payment Source.** The Stewardship Fee is paid from Company operating cash flow. It is **not** paid from ESOP trust assets or SBA loan proceeds. The Fee is disclosed to the SBA lender as a recurring operating expense in the debt service coverage ratio underwriting model.

**3.8 EBITDA Recalculation.** If the Company's actual EBITDA for any fiscal year differs by more than **15%** from the baseline, the parties shall negotiate in good faith to adjust the Fee prospectively. No retroactive adjustments shall apply.

## ARTICLE 4 — SERVICE SCOPE

**4.1 Service Pillars.** Forhemit shall provide the following stewardship services during the Term:

**P1 — Operational Continuity Monitoring.** Forhemit shall review the Company's Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP) on a monthly basis and provide a written status report within ten (10) business days of each month-end. The report shall summarize testing results, identified gaps, and remediation status. If no formal COOP exists at Closing, Forhemit shall develop a COOP framework within sixty (60) days.

**P2 — Key-Person Risk Tracking.** Forhemit shall monitor the employment status and succession readiness of **critical personnel** (defined as C-suite executives, top revenue producers, and persons with specialized institutional knowledge) on a quarterly basis. Forhemit shall maintain an escalation protocol for key-person departures and provide written notification to the Company within two (2) business days of any critical personnel change. Succession readiness criteria include: (a) identified successor; (b) documented knowledge transfer; and (c) interim coverage plan.

**P3 — Financial Baseline Reporting.** Forhemit shall prepare monthly comparison of actual financial results to the QofE baseline. EBITDA variance exceeding ±15% shall trigger immediate written notification to the Company. Forhemit shall coordinate with the Company's CPA for inclusion in lender reporting.

**P4 — Governance Documentation.** Forhemit shall maintain or update, as applicable: (a) operating procedures; (b) delegation of authority; (c) board resolution log; and (d) officer succession plan. Forhemit shall create draft documents for any item not existing at Closing within ninety (90) days.

**P5 — Lender Covenant Monitoring.** Forhemit shall track the Company's compliance with DSCR, liquidity, and CapEx covenants on a monthly basis. Forhemit shall provide the Company with sixty (60) days' advance written notice of any projected covenant breach using current run-rate and pipeline analysis.

**P6 — Succession Progress Reporting.** Forhemit shall provide quarterly updates on successor readiness. At the end of the stewardship period, Forhemit shall deliver the Final Transition Report.

**4.2 Company Cooperation.** The Company covenants to: (a) provide monthly financial statements within fifteen (15) days of month-end; (b) provide access to personnel records as needed for key-person tracking; (c) provide access to existing governance documents; and (d) execute lender information release authorizations.

**4.3 Service Exclusions.** Unless separately agreed in writing, Forhemit's services do **not** include: (a) tax preparation or tax advice; (b) legal services or legal opinions; (c) audit or review services; (d) executive search or recruitment; (e) day-to-day operational management; (f) banking relationship management; (g) trustee services or fiduciary advice; (h) ERP or system implementation; or (i) loan guaranty or personal guarantees.

**4.4 Final Transition Report.** The Final Transition Report shall include: (a) summary of stewardship activities completed; (b) current operational continuity status; (c) key-person succession assessment; (d) financial performance vs. baseline summary; (e) governance recommendations; (f) outstanding risks requiring attention; and (g) recommended ongoing service providers.

## ARTICLE 5 — STANDARD OF CARE

**5.1 Standard of Care.** Forhemit shall perform services in a commercially reasonable manner consistent with industry standards for post-transaction monitoring services. Forhemit exercises no discretionary authority over Company operations and makes no management decisions.

**5.2 No Guarantee of Results.** Forhemit does **not** guarantee or warrant any specific financial results, covenant compliance, ESOP plan performance, or business outcomes. The Company remains solely responsible for all operational and financial decisions.

**5.3 Reliance on Company Data.** Forhemit's services and reports rely on data, information, and representations provided by the Company, its management, and third-party professionals. Forhemit does not independently verify all such information and shall not be liable for errors or omissions in such third-party data.

## ARTICLE 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY

**6.1 Definition.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by either party to the other, including: (a) financial information, results, and projections; (b) business plans, strategies, and methods; (c) customer, supplier, and employee lists; (d) trade secrets and intellectual property; and (e) the existence and terms of this Agreement.

**6.2 Obligations.** Each party shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement; (c) protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information (and in no event less than reasonable care); and (d) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent, except to those employees, advisors, or agents who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein.

**6.3 Exceptions.** Confidential Information obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by the receiving party; (b) was in the receiving party's possession prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the receiving party; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order.

**6.4 Compelled Disclosure.** If a party is required by legal process to disclose Confidential Information, it shall provide prompt written notice to the other party to allow for a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

**6.5 Return of Materials.** Upon termination of this Agreement or at the disclosing party's request, each party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information of the other party in its possession.

**6.6 Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in this Article 6 shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of **three (3) years**.

Forhemit shall provide the Company with five (5) business days' notice before notifying the Lender, unless immediate notice is required to prevent imminent harm.

**7.6 Amendment Disclosure.** Any material amendment to this Agreement shall be disclosed to the Lender and Trustee within ten (10) days of execution.

## ARTICLE 8 — TERMINATION

**8.1 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** after receipt of written notice (or **ten (10) days** in the case of payment breaches); (b) fails to pay any amount when due and fails to cure within ten (10) days of written notice; (c) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) engages in fraud or willful misconduct relating to this Agreement.

**8.2 Termination for Convenience.**
- The **Company** may terminate this Agreement without cause upon **sixty (60) days'** prior written notice to Forhemit.
- **Forhemit** may terminate this Agreement without cause upon **ninety (90) days'** prior written notice to the Company.

**8.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason: (a) the Company shall pay Forhemit for all services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date; (b) upon termination for convenience, the Company shall pay a prorated portion of the then-current tranche fee calculated through the termination date; (c) upon termination for cause, all unpaid fees shall become immediately due; (d) Forhemit shall deliver all Company property and work product within ten (10) days; (e) Forhemit shall be entitled to payment for all work-in-process; and (f) each party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information.

**8.4 Survival.** Articles 3 (Fee, with respect to unpaid amounts), 6 (Confidentiality), 8 (Termination), 9 (Limitation of Liability), 10 (Indemnification), 11 (Dispute Resolution), and 12 (General Provisions) shall survive termination of this Agreement.

**9.2 Consequential Damages Waiver.** IN NO EVENT SHALL FORHEMIT BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, SPECIAL, INCIDENTAL, PUNITIVE, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, LOST BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES, OR DAMAGES ARISING FROM ESOP PLAN DISQUALIFICATION, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER FORHEMIT WAS ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**9.3 No Reliance.** The Company acknowledges that: (a) Forhemit is not making any guarantees or projections regarding the Company's financial performance; (b) the Stewardship Fee is based on historical EBITDA and does not guarantee future results; (c) Forhemit has no responsibility for the Company's ability to service its SBA loan or maintain ESOP compliance; and (d) the Company has relied on its own advisors and counsel in entering into this Agreement.
