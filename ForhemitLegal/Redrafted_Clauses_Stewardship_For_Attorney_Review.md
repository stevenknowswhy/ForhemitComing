## SECTION 3: STEWARDSHIP FEE

### 3.1 Enhanced Fee Clause (replaces lines 861-865)

```
3.1 Fee Calculation. The Company shall pay Forhemit an annual stewardship fee equal to 2.5% of the QofE Adjusted EBITDA as of the Closing Date (${ebitda}), which equals ${annual_fee} per year (the "Annual Fee"). The Quarterly Fee is ${quarterly_fee}.

3.2 Payment Schedule. The Fee shall be payable in tranches as follows:

(a) Tranche 1 (Months 1–6): ${t1_fee} (60% of Annual Fee) — due upon execution of this Agreement;
(b) Tranche 2 (Months 7–12): ${t2_fee} (20% of Annual Fee) — due on the date six months after the Effective Date;
(c) Tranche 3 (Months 13–18): ${t3_fee} (10% of Annual Fee) — due upon written election by both parties;
(d) Tranche 4 (Months 19–24): ${t4_fee} (10% of Annual Fee) — due upon written election by both parties.

3.3 Extension Election. Either party may elect to extend into Tranche 3 or Tranche 4 by providing written notice to the other party no later than thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then-current tranche period. If both parties elect to extend, the applicable tranche fee becomes due and payable within ten (10) days of such election. Either party may decline extension by providing written notice at least forty-five (45) days prior to the end of the then-current period. No party shall be obligated to agree to extension.

3.4 Payment Terms. All invoices are due and payable within thirty (30) days of receipt. Fees not paid when due shall accrue interest at the rate of 1.5% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less) from the due date until paid in full.

3.5 Suspension of Services. If any invoice remains unpaid thirty (30) days after receipt, Forhemit may suspend all services until payment is received in full, without liability to the Company. Forhemit may also withhold delivery of the Final Transition Report pending payment in full of all outstanding fees.

3.6 Expenses. The Fee does not include out-of-pocket expenses. Reasonable out-of-pocket expenses (including, without limitation, travel and third-party costs) shall be reimbursed by the Company upon presentation of receipts. Expenses exceeding $[AMOUNT] require prior written approval from the Company.

3.7 EBITDA Recalculation. If the Company's actual EBITDA for any fiscal year differs by more than [15]% from the baseline, the parties shall negotiate in good faith to adjust the Fee prospectively. No retroactive adjustments shall apply. For the avoidance of doubt, the Fee is calculated based on the baseline EBITDA and is not adjusted based on actual performance.
```

**Attorney Note**: Addresses payment timing, late fees, suspension rights, expenses, and EBITDA adjustment mechanism.

## SECTION 4: SERVICE SCOPE (NEW SECTION)

### 4.1 Service Pillars (NEW — to be added to PDF sections array)

```
4.1 Service Scope. Forhemit shall provide the stewardship services set forth in this Agreement. The Company has selected the following Service Pillars:

[INSERT SELECTED PILLARS FROM FORM]

P1 — Operational Continuity Monitoring: Forhemit shall review the Company's Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP) on a monthly basis and provide a written status report within ten (10) business days of each month-end. The report shall summarize testing results, identified gaps, and remediation status. If no formal COOP exists at Closing, Forhemit shall develop a COOP framework within sixty (60) days.

P2 — Key-Person Risk Tracking: Forhemit shall monitor the employment status and succession readiness of critical personnel (defined as C-suite executives, top revenue producers, and persons with specialized institutional knowledge) on a quarterly basis. Forhemit shall maintain an escalation protocol for key-person departures and provide written notification to the Company within two (2) business days of any critical personnel change.

P3 — Financial Baseline Reporting: Forhemit shall prepare monthly comparison of actual financial results to the QofE baseline. EBITDA variance exceeding ±15% shall trigger immediate written notification to the Company. Forhemit shall coordinate with the Company's CPA for inclusion in lender reporting. The Company shall deliver monthly financial statements within fifteen (15) days of month-end.

P4 — Governance Documentation: Forhemit shall maintain or update, as applicable: (a) operating procedures; (b) delegation of authority; (c) board resolution log; and (d) officer succession plan. Forhemit shall create draft documents for any item not existing at Closing within ninety (90) days.

P5 — Lender Covenant Monitoring: Forhemit shall track the Company's compliance with DSCR, liquidity, and CapEx covenants on a monthly basis. Forhemit shall provide the Company with sixty (60) days' advance written notice of any projected covenant breach using current run-rate and pipeline analysis. Forhemit shall deliver monthly covenant summaries to the designated SBA lender relationship manager.

P6 — Succession Progress Reporting: Forhemit shall provide quarterly updates on successor readiness. Successor readiness criteria include: (a) identified successor; (b) documented knowledge transfer; and (c) interim coverage plan. At the end of the stewardship period, Forhemit shall deliver the Final Transition Report.

4.2 Company Cooperation. The Company covenants to: (a) provide monthly financial statements within fifteen (15) days of month-end; (b) provide access to personnel records as needed for key-person tracking; (c) provide access to existing governance documents; and (d) execute lender information release authorizations.

4.3 Service Exclusions. Unless separately agreed in writing, Forhemit's services do not include: (a) tax preparation or tax advice; (b) legal services or legal opinions; (c) audit or review services; (d) executive search or recruitment; (e) day-to-day operational management; (f) banking relationship management; (g) trustee services or fiduciary advice; (h) ERP or system implementation; or (i) loan guaranty or personal guarantees.

4.4 Final Transition Report. The Final Transition Report shall include: (a) summary of stewardship activities completed; (b) current operational continuity status; (c) key-person succession assessment; (d) financial performance vs. baseline summary; (e) governance recommendations; (f) outstanding risks requiring attention; and (g) recommended ongoing service providers.
```

## SECTION 5: LENDER & TRUSTEE DISCLOSURE

### 5.1 Enhanced Disclosure Clause (replaces lines 866-869)

```
5.1 Lender Disclosure. The Company shall provide a complete copy of this Agreement to ${lender_name} (the "Lender") prior to the Effective Date. The Company confirms that the Stewardship Fee is included in the Lender's DSCR underwriting model as a recurring operating expense of the Company.

5.2 Trustee Disclosure and Acknowledgment. Forhemit shall deliver a complete copy of this Agreement and a separate ERISA Section 408(b)(2) disclosure statement to ${trustee_name} (the "Trustee") prior to the Effective Date. The Section 408(b)(2) disclosure shall include: (a) description of all services to be provided; (b) schedule of all compensation, direct and indirect; (c) statement regarding Forhemit's non-fiduciary status; and (d) confirmation that Forhemit has no policies affecting trustee decision-making. The Trustee's signature on this Agreement acknowledges receipt only and creates no obligation or liability on behalf of the Trustee or the ESOP.

5.3 ERISA Status. Forhemit is not a fiduciary within the meaning of ERISA Section 3(21) with respect to the ESOP or its assets. Forhemit's role is limited to providing services to the Company as an independent contractor. Forhemit provides services solely to the Company as an operating entity, not to the ESOP trust or plan. Forhemit has no authority to direct investments, distributions, or trustee decisions regarding plan assets.

5.4 Amendment Disclosure. Any material amendment to this Agreement shall be disclosed to the Lender and Trustee within ten (10) days of execution.

5.5 Material Risk Definition. For purposes of lender notification, "material risk" means: (a) actual or projected DSCR below 1.25x for two consecutive months; (b) actual or projected covenant breach; (c) loss of key person without qualified successor; or (d) any condition that, in Forhemit's reasonable judgment, could result in SBA loan acceleration or material adverse change in the Company's operations. Forhemit shall provide the Company with five (5) business days' notice before notifying the Lender, unless immediate notice is required to prevent imminent harm.
```

## SECTION 7: CONFIDENTIALITY (NEW)

### 7.1 Confidentiality Provision (NEW)

```
7.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by either party to the other, including without limitation: (a) financial information, results, and projections; (b) business plans, strategies, and methods; (c) customer, supplier, and employee lists; (d) trade secrets and intellectual property; and (e) the existence and terms of this Agreement.

7.2 Obligations. Each party shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement; (c) protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information (and in no event less than reasonable care); and (d) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent, except to those employees, advisors, or agents who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those contained herein.

7.3 Exceptions. Confidential Information obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by the receiving party; (b) was in the receiving party's possession prior to disclosure by the disclosing party; (c) is independently developed by the receiving party without use of or reference to Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order.

7.4 Compelled Disclosure. If a party is required by legal process to disclose Confidential Information, it shall provide prompt written notice to the other party to allow for a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

7.5 Return of Materials. Upon termination of this Agreement or at the disclosing party's request, each party shall return or, at the disclosing party's option, destroy all Confidential Information of the other party in its possession, custody, or control.

7.6 Survival. The confidentiality obligations in this Section 7 shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.
```

**Attorney Note**: Standard mutual confidentiality provision with survival period.

## SECTION 8: TERMINATION (NEW)

### 8.1 Termination Provision (NEW)

```
8.1 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice if the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days after receipt of written notice (or ten (10) days in the case of payment breaches); (b) fails to pay any amount when due and fails to cure within ten (10) days of written notice; (c) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, has a receiver appointed, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors; or (d) engages in fraud or willful misconduct relating to this Agreement.

8.2 Termination for Convenience. The Company may terminate this Agreement without cause upon sixty (60) days' prior written notice to Forhemit. Forhemit may terminate this Agreement without cause upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the Company.

8.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason: (a) the Company shall pay Forhemit for all services performed and expenses incurred through the termination date; (b) upon termination for convenience, the Company shall pay a prorated portion of the then-current tranche fee calculated through the termination date; (c) upon termination for cause, all unpaid fees shall become immediately due; (d) Forhemit shall deliver all Company property and work product within ten (10) days; (e) Forhemit shall be entitled to payment for all work-in-process; and (f) each party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information.

8.4 Survival. Sections 3 (Fee, with respect to unpaid amounts), 7 (Confidentiality), 8 (Termination), 9 (Limitation of Liability), 10 (Indemnification), 11 (Dispute Resolution), and 12 (General Provisions) shall survive termination of this Agreement.
```

**Attorney Note**: Comprehensive termination framework addressing both cause and convenience termination.

## SECTION 10: INDEMNIFICATION (NEW)

### 10.1 Indemnification Provision (NEW)

```
10.1 Indemnification by Company. The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Forhemit and its officers, directors, members, employees, agents, and affiliates (the "Forhemit Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all losses, damages, liabilities, deficiencies, claims, actions, judgments, settlements, interest, awards, penalties, fines, costs, or expenses of whatever kind (including reasonable attorneys' fees and costs of investigation and litigation) arising out of or related to: (a) the Company's breach of this Agreement; (b) the Company's operations, financial condition, or compliance with its obligations; (c) any third-party claim relating to the Company's business; or (d) any allegation that Forhemit's services constitute fiduciary advice under ERISA, except to the extent such claim results from Forhemit's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

10.2 Indemnification by Forhemit. Forhemit shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company and its officers, directors, members, employees, and agents (the "Company Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all losses arising out of or related to: (a) Forhemit's material breach of this Agreement; (b) Forhemit's gross negligence or willful misconduct; or (c) Forhemit's misappropriation of the Company's Confidential Information.

10.3 Indemnification Procedure. The party seeking indemnification (the "Indemnified Party") shall: (a) provide prompt written notice of the claim to the indemnifying party (the "Indemnifying Party"); (b) allow the Indemnifying Party to control the defense and settlement of the claim; and (c) reasonably cooperate in the defense at the Indemnifying Party's expense. The Indemnifying Party may not settle any claim without the Indemnified Party's prior written consent if such settlement involves an admission of fault, financial liability not covered by the Indemnifying Party, or equitable relief against the Indemnified Party.
```

**Attorney Note**: Mutual indemnification with Company assuming primary indemnity obligation (standard for service agreements).

### 12.1 General Provisions (NEW)

```
12.1 Notices. All notices, requests, consents, claims, demands, waivers, and other communications hereunder shall be in writing and shall be deemed to have been given: (a) when delivered by hand; (b) when received by the addressee if sent by a nationally recognized overnight courier; (c) on the date sent by email if sent during normal business hours of the recipient; or (d) on the third day after the date mailed, by certified or registered mail, return receipt requested, postage prepaid. 12.7 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall be deemed to be one and the same agreement. Electronic signatures shall have the same force and effect as original signatures.

12.8 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for any delay or failure in performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, natural disasters, pandemics, government actions, or internet failures, provided that the affected party gives prompt written notice to the other and uses commercially reasonable efforts to resume performance.
```
