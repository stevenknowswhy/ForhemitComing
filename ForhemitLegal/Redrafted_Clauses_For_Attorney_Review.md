## 1. TAIL PROVISION

### Current Language (Line 563)
> "If the company closes a transaction within 180 days of termination with a team substantially assembled by Forhemit, the success fee is owed."

### Suggested Revision

```
TAIL PROVISION

If the Company closes a transaction with any person or entity that (i) was introduced to
the Company by Forhemit during the term of this Agreement, or (ii) was identified as a
prospective buyer or investor by Forhemit during the term, within 180 days following the
termination or expiration of this Agreement, then the Transition Success Fee shall be
payable as if this Agreement had remained in effect through the closing date.

The foregoing obligation shall only apply if Forhemit provides the Company with written
notice of such introduction or identification prior to termination. For the avoidance of
doubt, a transaction with a party not introduced or identified by Forhemit shall not
trigger this Tail Provision, regardless of whether Forhemit assembled a team or performed
services for the Company.

The tail provision shall not apply if this Agreement is terminated by Company due to
Forhemit's material breach or willful misconduct.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Defines "introduced" and "identified" parties only
- Adds notice requirement
- Adds termination for cause exception
- Removes undefined "substantially assembled" language

## 2. RETAINER CLAUSE

### Current Language (Line 365)
> "The $25,000 retainer is non-refundable under all circumstances"

### Suggested Revision

```
RETAINER

The Company agrees to pay Forhemit a non-refundable retainer in the amount of $25,000.00
(the "Retainer"). The Retainer shall be deemed earned by Forhemit upon execution of this
Agreement by both parties and shall be paid by the Company from its operating account
within five (5) business days of such execution. The Retainer does not apply toward any
Transition Success Fee or other fees payable hereunder.

The Retainer is non-refundable except in the event that Forhemit materially breaches this
Agreement, fails to substantially perform the services contemplated herein, or the
Agreement is terminated by Company prior to Forhemit's commencement of material services.
In any such event, the Retainer shall be pro-rated based on services actually rendered and
the unearned portion shall be refunded to the Company.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Defines "execution" clearly
- Adds material breach exception
- Adds pro-rating for early termination
- Clarifies 5-business-day payment window

## 4. §1042 TAX ELECTION WARNING

### Replace: Current S-Corp warning (Lines 448-450)

### Suggested Revision

```
SECTION 1042 ROLLOVER WARNING

NOTICE: Section 1042 of the Internal Revenue Code may allow qualifying sellers to defer
capital gains tax by reinvesting sale proceeds in Qualified Replacement Property (QRP),
such as securities of domestic operating corporations. HOWEVER, this election is ONLY
available if the selling entity is a C-Corporation at the time of sale. S-Corporations
DO NOT qualify.

If the Company is currently taxed as an S-Corporation and wishes to access §1042 deferral,
conversion to C-Corporation status is required BEFORE the sale. This conversion has
PERMANENT tax consequences:

1. DOUBLE TAXATION: All future earnings will be subject to corporate-level tax and
   shareholder-level tax (currently 21% corporate + up to 20% qualified dividend rate).

2. BUILT-IN GAINS TAX: If the company was previously an S-Corporation, built-in gains
   tax may apply for up to five years after conversion.

3. LOSS OF PASS-THROUGH: Losses, deductions, and credits will no longer pass through to
   shareholders.

4. STATE TAX IMPACT: State-level tax treatment will change and may result in higher
   overall taxes.

5. IRREVERSIBLE: Conversion from S-Corporation to C-Corporation is permanent.
   Reconversion to S-Corporation is generally not available for at least five years
   and may not be available at all under IRS rules.

This warning is provided for informational purposes only. Forhemit does NOT provide tax
advice and makes NO recommendation regarding §1042 election or S-Corporation conversion.
Company MUST consult with qualified tax counsel (CPA and/or tax attorney) to analyze
whether §1042 benefits outweigh permanent C-Corporation tax burden BEFORE executing any
Letter of Intent.

[ ] Company acknowledges receipt and understanding of this warning
[ ] Company confirms tax counsel has been consulted regarding §1042
```

### Attorney Notes
- Comprehensive warning of permanent consequences
- Checkboxes for client acknowledgment
- Clear statement that Forhemit provides no tax advice
- Should be reviewed by tax specialist

## 5. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### Insert As: New Section

### Suggested Revision

```
LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

NOTWITHSTANDING ANYTHING TO THE CONTRARY HEREIN, EXCEPT FOR (A) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY
OBLIGATIONS, (B) WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (C) GROSS NEGLIGENCE, FORHEMIT'S TOTAL AGGREGATE
LIABILITY TO COMPANY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES ACTUALLY PAID TO
FORHEMIT HEREUNDER.

IN NO EVENT SHALL FORHEMIT BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL,
OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, LOST BUSINESS OPPORTUNITIES,
OR DAMAGES ARISING FROM LOST DATA, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER SUCH DAMAGES WERE FORESEEABLE
AND REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY (CONTRACT, TORT, OR OTHERWISE).

FORHEMIT SHALL NOT BE LIABLE FOR ANY DECISIONS MADE BY COMPANY, ANY THIRD PARTY (INCLUDING
LENDERS, INVESTORS, BROKERS, ATTORNEYS, OR OTHER ADVISORS), OR ANY TRANSACTION OUTCOMES,
VALUATIONS, OR TERMS. COMPANY ACKNOWLEDGES THAT FORHEMIT PROVIDES COORDINATION AND
STEWARDSHIP SERVICES ONLY AND DOES NOT CONTROL OR GUARANTEE TRANSACTION RESULTS.

The limitations set forth in this Section shall not apply to any obligation to indemnify
arising under this Agreement.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Caps liability at fees paid
- Carve-outs for willful misconduct and gross negligence
- Excludes consequential damages
- Clarifies scope of services
- Does NOT limit indemnification obligations

## 6. INDEMNIFICATION

### Insert As: New Section

### Suggested Revision

```
INDEMNIFICATION

6.1 Company Indemnification. Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Forhemit
and its officers, directors, members, employees, agents, and affiliates (collectively,
"Forhemet Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all losses, damages, liabilities,
deficiencies, claims, actions, judgments, settlements, interest, awards, penalties, fines,
costs, or expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and expenses) (collectively,
"Losses") arising out of or relating to any third-party claim, suit, action, or proceeding
("Third-Party Claim") to the extent such Third-Party Claim arises out of or results from:
(a) any material breach by Company of any representation, warranty, or covenant in this
Agreement; (b) the gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud of Company or any of its
officers, directors, employees, or agents; or (c) any claim that the services provided by
Forhemit infringe upon the intellectual property rights of any third party to the extent
caused by materials or information provided by Company.

6.2 Forhemit Indemnification. Forhemit shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company
and its officers, directors, members, employees, agents, and affiliates from and against
any Losses arising out of or relating to any Third-Party Claim to the extent such
Third-Party Claim arises out of or results from Forhemit's gross negligence, willful
misconduct, or material breach of confidentiality obligations under this Agreement.

Forhemit shall have no indemnification obligation under this Section 6.2 for claims
arising from: (a) information or materials provided by Company or any third party;
(b) actions or omissions of any lender, broker, attorney, accountant, valuation firm,
trustee, or other third-party advisor; (c) Company's business decisions or actions; or
(d) any transaction outcome, valuation, or terms.

6.3 Indemnification Procedure. A party seeking indemnification ("Indemnified Party") shall
provide prompt written notice to the other party ("Indemnifying Party") of any Third-Party
Claim for which indemnification is sought. The Indemnifying Party shall have the right
to assume the defense of such Third-Party Claim with counsel of its choice reasonably
acceptable to the Indemnified Party. The Indemnified Party may participate in such defense
at its own expense. The Indemnifying Party shall not settle any Third-Party Claim without
the prior written consent of the Indemnified Party, which shall not be unreasonably
withheld.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Mutual indemnification with asymmetrical obligations
- Company indemnifies Forhemit broadly
- Forhemit indemnifies Company only for actual misconduct
- Includes indemnification procedures
- Consider whether duty to defend language is appropriate

## 7. NO RESULTS DISCLAIMER

### Insert Into: Section 2 (Scope)

### Suggested Revision

```
NO RESULTS DISCLAIMER

Company acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) Forhemit does not and cannot guaranty any specific transaction outcome, including
    but not limited to, successful closing, financing terms, valuation, purchase price,
    timing, or post-transaction integration;

(b) The ultimate success of any transaction depends on numerous factors outside Forhemit's
    control, including without limitation market conditions, lender decisions and approvals,
    regulatory requirements, Company's financial performance, third-party actions, and
    economic conditions;

(c) Forhemit makes no representations or warranties, express or implied, regarding the
    likelihood of transaction success, the achievability of any specific timeline, or the
    availability of financing on any particular terms; and

(d) The success fee is earned upon consummation of a transaction, not upon achievement of
    any particular terms, valuation, financing structure, or seller proceeds.

Company acknowledges that it is relying on its own judgment and advice from its own
independent advisors in deciding to pursue any transaction.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Comprehensive no-guaranty language
- Lists factors outside Forhemit's control
- Clarifies fee trigger
- Emphasizes client's reliance on its own advisors

## 10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

## 11. FORCE MAJEURE

### Insert As: New Section

### Suggested Revision

```
FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party shall be liable or responsible to the other Party, nor be deemed to have
defaulted under or breached this Agreement, for any failure or delay in fulfilling or
performing any term of this Agreement (except for payment obligations), when and to the
extent such failure or delay is caused by or results from acts or circumstances beyond
the reasonable control of the affected Party, including without limitation: acts of God;
floods, fires, earthquakes, or other natural disasters; war, terrorism, or civil unrest;
government actions, restrictions, or regulations; epidemics, pandemics, or quarantines;
labor strikes or slowdowns; or other similar events beyond the affected Party's reasonable
control (each, a "Force Majeure Event").

The affected Party shall give prompt written notice to the other Party of the Force Majeure
Event and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to cure the delay caused by such
Force Majeure Event. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than 60 days, either Party
may terminate this Agreement upon written notice to the other Party.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Comprehensive list of force majeure events
- Includes pandemics explicitly
- Notice requirement
- Good faith cure obligation
- 60-day termination right

```

Any broker or M&A advisor identified by Company acts independently of Forhemit. Forhemit's
role is limited to transaction stewardship and coordination services described in Section 2.
```

## 14. CONFIDENTIALITY

### Insert As: New Section

### Suggested Revision

```
CONFIDENTIALITY

14.1 Definition. As used in this Section, "Confidential Information" means any non-public
information, whether in tangible or intangible form, disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing
Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party") in connection with this Agreement,
including without limitation: financial information, business plans, operational data,
customer lists, transaction structures, financing arrangements, and the existence and terms
of this Agreement.

14.2 Obligations. The Receiving Party agrees that: (a) it shall maintain as confidential
and shall not disclose to any third party any Confidential Information without the prior
written consent of the Disclosing Party; (b) it shall use Confidential Information only for
purposes of this Agreement; and (c) it shall restrict disclosure of Confidential Information
to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know such information and
are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained herein.

14.3 Exclusions. Confidential Information shall not include information that: (a) is or
becomes generally available to the public other than as a result of a disclosure by the
Receiving Party; (b) was in the Receiving Party's possession prior to disclosure by the
Disclosing Party; (c) becomes available to the Receiving Party on a non-confidential basis
from a source other than the Disclosing Party; or (d) is independently developed by the
Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

14.4 Survival. The obligations of this Section shall survive the termination or expiration
of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

14.5 Compelled Disclosure. If a Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or legal
process to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall provide the Disclosing Party with
prompt written notice of such requirement to permit the Disclosing Party to seek a protective
order or other appropriate remedy.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Standard mutual confidentiality provision
- Defines Confidential Information broadly
- Standard exclusions
- 3-year survival period
- Compelled disclosure notice requirement

## 15. TERMINATION

### Insert As: New Section

### Suggested Revision

```
TERMINATION

15.1 Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon written notice
to the other Party if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure
such breach within 15 days after receipt of written notice thereof. If the breach is not
capable of cure within 15 days, the breaching Party shall not be entitled to any cure
period.

15.2 Forhemit's Right to Suspend. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, Forhemit may
suspend services immediately without notice if: (a) Company fails to pay any amount when
due; (b) Company provides false or misleading material information; or (c) Company breaches
any confidentiality obligation.

15.3 Effect of Termination. Upon termination for cause: (a) the non-breaching Party shall
be entitled to all remedies available at law or in equity; (b) the Company shall remain
liable for all fees and expenses incurred through the termination date; and (c) the tail
provision in Section ___ shall apply according to its terms.

15.4 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon 30 days'
prior written notice to the other Party. In the event of termination for convenience by
Company, the Retainer shall be non-refundable and Company shall remain liable for all
out-of-pocket expenses incurred by Forhemit through the termination date.
```

### Attorney Notes
- Termination for cause with cure period
- Forhemit's immediate suspension right
- Clarifies effect on fees and expenses
- Adds termination for convenience (optional)
